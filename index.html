Mortgages 101
Introductory questions
#1
Does a lower rate always lead to lower monthly payments?
No. Your payments are impacted by your rate, but also by your total loan amount and loan term.

A 30-year loan at a 5% rate will have lower monthly payments than a 15-yr loan at a 4.9% rate for example, but with the 30-yr loan, you'll be making payments for 15 more years.
#2
How should I decide what rate and term to select?
It depends on your goals.

If you want the lowest monthly payments then choose the longers terms.

If you want the lowest rate and lowest interest costs over time, choose the shortest term.
#3
So which should I care about, a low rate or low payments?
If you have investment opportunities that can earn a risk-adjust return greater than your mortgage payments, like stocks or real-estate, then go with the longer-term loan and lower payments. The difference between what you'd be paying can be socked away in these high return investments. This maximizes your total investment income.

If you don't have good investment options, then go with the shortest term mortgage you can afford and pay that down. This minimizes your total interest payments.
#4
How do I get the lowest fees?
Shop around for different lenders and get official Loan Estiamtes from each one. Compare the fees. If two lenders have the same loan amount, date, and other terms, you'll have an apples-to-apples comparison.
#5
How do I know if now is the right time to get a mortgage?
For a refi, use any decent online refi calculator like this Zillow calculator.

For a home loan, it's more complex. Decide whether you can afford to make the monthly payments and are willing to go through the work of finding and executing a purchase transaction.
